AI works best for me when it behaves like leverage, not authority.

## Good use cases
The strongest wins usually come from:

- faster research loops
- quick implementation scaffolds
- debugging support
- test and documentation acceleration
- comparing possible approaches before committing to one

## What I do not outsource
I do not want final product decisions, architecture boundaries, or review standards to become accidental outputs from a tool.

Those are still engineering responsibilities.

## The actual goal
The goal is not to say “AI built this.” The goal is to reduce low-value friction so I can spend more time on:

- tradeoffs
- architecture quality
- UX details
- maintainability
- communication with stakeholders

That is the workflow I want to show in a portfolio too: modern, fast, and still deliberate.
